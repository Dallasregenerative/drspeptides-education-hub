## 2. Deep Mechanism of Action

Formula RG-5555 integrates four synergistic peptides that together orchestrate a powerful, multi-faceted healing response, addressing the root causes of poor recovery.

### 2.1 Larazotide: Foundational – Gut Barrier Restoration

Larazotide is a synthetic peptide that acts as a zonulin antagonist. Zonulin is a protein that reversibly regulates intestinal permeability. In many inflammatory states, zonulin levels are elevated, leading to the breakdown of tight junctions between intestinal cells (a "leaky gut"). This allows bacterial endotoxins (LPS) and undigested food particles to enter the bloodstream, triggering a state of chronic, low-grade systemic inflammation. By blocking zonulin, Larazotide restores the integrity of the intestinal barrier, effectively shutting off this primary source of systemic inflammation [4].

### 2.2 KPV: Local – Potent Anti-Inflammatory Action

KPV is the C-terminal fragment of α-MSH and is one of the most potent anti-inflammatory peptides known. It acts intracellularly to inhibit the activation of Nuclear Factor-kappa B (NF-κB), a master regulator of the inflammatory response. By preventing NF-κB from entering the nucleus, KPV effectively shuts down the transcription and production of numerous pro-inflammatory cytokines (e.g., TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6) directly at the site of injury or inflammation [3].

### 2.3 TB-500 (as Ac-SDKP): Cellular Migration & Differentiation

TB-500 is a synthetic version of Thymosin Beta-4, a naturally occurring peptide that plays a key role in tissue repair. Its primary mechanism is to sequester actin monomers, promoting the rapid polymerization of the actin cytoskeleton, which is essential for cell motility. This action promotes the migration of stem cells, endothelial cells, and keratinocytes to the site of injury. It also has anti-inflammatory properties and promotes angiogenesis, creating a favorable environment for healing [2].

### 2.4 BPC-157: Systemic Tissue Repair & Angiogenesis

BPC-157 (Body Protective Compound) is a pentadecapeptide with extraordinary regenerative capabilities. It is believed to be a master healing peptide that modulates the activity of numerous growth factors (e.g., VEGF, EGF) and signaling pathways. It potently promotes angiogenesis (the formation of new blood vessels), which is critical for delivering oxygen and nutrients to damaged tissue. It also accelerates the repair of tendon, ligament, muscle, bone, and nervous tissue, and has been shown to counteract the catabolic effects of corticosteroids [1].
