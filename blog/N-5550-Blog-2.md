Modern neuroscience is revealing a powerful truth about weight management: our body weight is tightly regulated by a complex interplay of neurotransmitters in the brain, particularly dopamine, serotonin, and norepinephrine. When you lose weight, your brain interprets this as a threat to survival and initiates a powerful counter-attack to push your weight back up to its “set point.”

This counter-attack includes:

-   **A surge in appetite and cravings.**
-   **A drop in motivation and mood.**
-   **A decrease in your metabolic rate.**

Sound familiar? This is the biological reality of the weight loss plateau. You’re not weak; you’re fighting against a brain that is hardwired to keep you from losing weight.

1.  **Dopamine:** By boosting dopamine, Tesofensine directly combats the drop in motivation and reward sensitivity that occurs during dieting. It helps you feel good, stay focused, and resist cravings.

2.  **Serotonin:** Increased serotonin is a powerful satiety signal, making you feel full and satisfied with less food.

3.  **Norepinephrine:** This neurotransmitter increases your resting energy expenditure, effectively turning up your metabolic thermostat and helping you burn more calories.

By targeting all three of these pathways simultaneously, Tesofensine effectively “tricks” the brain into accepting a new, lower body weight set point. It short-circuits the brain’s starvation response, allowing you to continue losing weight without the constant battle against cravings and low energy.
