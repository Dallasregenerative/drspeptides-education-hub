## What is Leaky Gut?

Your intestinal lining is an incredibly sophisticated barrier. It’s designed to allow beneficial nutrients to pass into your bloodstream while keeping harmful substances—like undigested food particles, toxins, and bacterial components—out. This barrier is maintained by a network of proteins called tight junctions.

In a healthy gut, these tight junctions are strong and secure. But due to factors like a poor diet, chronic stress, or certain infections, these junctions can become loose and permeable. This is “leaky gut.”

When the gut is leaky, a potent inflammatory molecule from bacteria called **Lipopolysaccharide (LPS)** can “leak” into your bloodstream. Your immune system sees LPS as a major threat and launches a powerful, body-wide inflammatory response.

## The Systemic Fire of a Leaky Gut

This creates a state of chronic, low-grade systemic inflammation. Think of it as a low-level fire that is constantly smoldering throughout your entire body. This systemic fire has two devastating effects on injury recovery:

1.  **It Diverts Resources:** Your immune system is a finite resource. When it’s busy fighting a constant, low-grade war against the inflammatory molecules leaking from your gut, it has fewer resources available to mount a focused, effective healing response at the site of your actual injury.

2.  **It Amplifies Local Inflammation:** The systemic inflammation from your gut adds fuel to the fire of your local injury. It makes the inflammation in your injured shoulder more intense, more painful, and longer-lasting.

In short, a leaky gut sabotages your body’s ability to heal efficiently.
