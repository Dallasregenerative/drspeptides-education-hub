In the world of peptide therapy, the term "stack" has become ubiquitous. It refers to a pre-defined combination of peptides aimed at a general goal, like weight loss or anti-aging. While seemingly convenient, this one-size-fits-all approach is fundamentally flawed and may be doing a disservice to your patients.

## The Problem with Generic Stacks

1.  **They Ignore Individuality:** Every patient is unique. Their genetics, health history, lifestyle, and goals are different. A generic stack for "weight loss" fails to consider if the patient is insulin resistant, has thyroid issues, or is a post-menopausal woman. This leads to suboptimal results.

2.  **They Disregard Contraindications:** A popular peptide in a generic stack might be contraindicated for a patient with a specific medical condition or who is taking a certain medication. Without a personalized screening process, you risk patient safety.

3.  **They Use Arbitrary Dosing:** Generic stacks often come with generic dosing recommendations that aren't based on patient weight, lab results, or clinical evidence. This can lead to ineffective treatment or unnecessary side effects.

4.  **They Create a False Sense of Simplicity:** While easy to prescribe, generic stacks can lead to complex problems when they don't work or cause adverse effects. This erodes patient trust and satisfaction.
