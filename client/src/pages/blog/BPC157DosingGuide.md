# BPC-157 Dosing & Administration: The Complete Practitioner's Protocol Guide

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) is a synthetic pentadecapeptide derived from human gastric juice that has demonstrated remarkable healing properties across multiple organ systems. As one of the most widely prescribed therapeutic peptides in regenerative medicine, practitioners need a comprehensive understanding of dosing strategies, administration routes, and clinical decision-making.

This guide provides evidence-based protocols for BPC-157 administration, drawing from published research, clinical experience, and emerging best practices in peptide therapy.

## Understanding BPC-157 Pharmacology

BPC-157 is a 15-amino acid peptide (Gly-Glu-Pro-Pro-Pro-Gly-Lys-Pro-Ala-Asp-Asp-Ala-Gly-Leu-Val) that exerts its effects through multiple mechanisms [1]:

- **Nitric oxide system modulation** — promotes angiogenesis and blood vessel repair
- **Growth factor upregulation** — increases VEGF, EGF, and other healing factors
- **Anti-inflammatory action** — modulates cytokine production
- **Gut-brain axis interaction** — influences dopaminergic and serotonergic systems
- **Tendon and ligament repair** — promotes fibroblast proliferation and collagen synthesis

Unlike many peptides, BPC-157 is remarkably stable in gastric acid (it is derived from gastric juice), which makes it one of the few peptides effective via oral administration [2].

## Standard Dosing Protocols

### Weight-Based Dosing

The most evidence-supported approach uses weight-based dosing:

| Patient Weight | Low Dose | Standard Dose | High Dose |
|----------------|----------|---------------|-----------|
| 50-70 kg (110-154 lbs) | 200 mcg/day | 300 mcg/day | 500 mcg/day |
| 70-90 kg (154-198 lbs) | 250 mcg/day | 400 mcg/day | 600 mcg/day |
| 90-110 kg (198-242 lbs) | 300 mcg/day | 500 mcg/day | 750 mcg/day |
| >110 kg (>242 lbs) | 400 mcg/day | 600 mcg/day | 1000 mcg/day |

**Research basis:** Most animal studies use 10 mcg/kg body weight, which translates to approximately 700-1000 mcg/day for an average adult. Clinical practitioners typically use 250-500 mcg/day with excellent results [3].

### Condition-Specific Protocols

#### Musculoskeletal Injuries (Tendons, Ligaments, Joints)

- **Dose:** 250-500 mcg/day subcutaneous
- **Injection site:** As close to the injury as possible
- **Duration:** 4-8 weeks
- **Frequency:** Once daily (can split to BID for severe injuries)
- **Cycling:** 4 weeks on, 2 weeks off, repeat if needed

#### Gastrointestinal Healing (IBS, Leaky Gut, Ulcers)

- **Dose:** 250-500 mcg/day oral (preferred) or subcutaneous
- **Timing:** On empty stomach, 30 minutes before meals
- **Duration:** 6-12 weeks for chronic conditions
- **Frequency:** Once or twice daily
- **Note:** Oral BPC-157 is specifically preferred for GI conditions due to local action

#### Post-Surgical Recovery

- **Dose:** 500 mcg BID (twice daily)
- **Injection site:** Near surgical site (not into the wound)
- **Duration:** 2-4 weeks post-surgery
- **Start:** 24-48 hours post-procedure (after hemostasis confirmed)
- **Combine with:** TB-500 for synergistic healing

#### Neurological Applications (TBI, Neuroprotection)

- **Dose:** 250-500 mcg/day subcutaneous (abdominal)
- **Duration:** 8-12 weeks
- **Frequency:** Once daily
- **Note:** Systemic administration preferred for CNS effects

#### GLP-1 Agonist GI Side Effect Management

- **Dose:** 250 mcg/day oral
- **Timing:** 30 minutes before the meal most associated with nausea
- **Duration:** Throughout GLP-1 therapy as needed
- **Rationale:** BPC-157 protects gastric mucosa and reduces inflammation

## Administration Routes

### Subcutaneous Injection (Most Common)

**Equipment:**
- Insulin syringe (29-31 gauge, 1/2 inch needle)
- Alcohol swabs
- Reconstituted BPC-157 vial

**Technique:**
1. Clean injection site with alcohol, allow to dry completely
2. Pinch skin fold at injection site
3. Insert needle at 45-90 degree angle
4. Inject slowly over 5-10 seconds
5. Hold for 5 seconds before withdrawing
6. Do not massage the area

**Injection Site Selection:**
- **For localized injuries:** Inject within 2-3 cm of the injury site
- **For systemic effects:** Abdominal subcutaneous fat (rotate quadrants)
- **For GI conditions:** Abdominal injection or oral administration

### Oral Administration

**When to use oral:**
- GI conditions (IBS, leaky gut, ulcers, GERD)
- Patient needle phobia
- Maintenance therapy after initial injectable course
- GLP-1 GI side effect management

**Oral dosing:**
- Capsule form: 250-500 mcg on empty stomach
- Sublingual: Hold under tongue for 60-90 seconds (enhanced absorption)
- Timing: 30 minutes before first meal of the day

**Bioavailability note:** Oral BPC-157 has approximately 50-70% bioavailability compared to injection for systemic effects, but may have HIGHER local bioavailability for GI conditions due to direct mucosal contact [4].

### Intramuscular Injection (Less Common)

Reserved for deep tissue injuries or when subcutaneous injection cannot reach the target area. Use a 25-27 gauge, 1-inch needle. Same dosing as subcutaneous.

## Reconstitution Guide

### Standard Reconstitution

**For a 5mg vial:**

| Bacteriostatic Water Added | Concentration per 0.1 mL (10 units) |
|----------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| 1 mL | 500 mcg |
| 2 mL | 250 mcg |
| 2.5 mL | 200 mcg |
| 5 mL | 100 mcg |

**Recommended:** Add 2 mL bacteriostatic water to a 5mg vial = 250 mcg per 10 units on an insulin syringe. This makes dosing intuitive and reduces calculation errors.

### Reconstitution Steps

1. Remove caps from both BPC-157 vial and bacteriostatic water
2. Swab both rubber stoppers with alcohol
3. Draw desired volume of bacteriostatic water into syringe
4. Inject bacteriostatic water into BPC-157 vial slowly, directing stream against the glass wall (NOT directly onto the powder)
5. Gently swirl vial until powder is fully dissolved — NEVER shake
6. Solution should be clear and colorless
7. Label vial with date, concentration, and expiration (28 days)

### Storage After Reconstitution

- **Refrigerated (2-8°C):** Stable for 28 days
- **Room temperature:** Maximum 72 hours (not recommended for routine storage)
- **Frozen:** Not recommended after reconstitution (may denature)
- **Light:** Store in original box or wrapped in foil (light-sensitive)
- **Travel:** Use insulated cooler with ice packs for transport

## Cycling Strategies

### Standard Cycling Protocol

**4 weeks on / 2 weeks off**

This is the most common cycling approach:
- Weeks 1-4: Full dose daily
- Weeks 5-6: Off (allows receptor resensitization)
- Repeat if condition requires additional treatment

### Extended Protocol (Chronic Conditions)

**8 weeks on / 4 weeks off**

For chronic GI conditions or long-standing injuries:
- Weeks 1-8: Full dose daily
- Weeks 9-12: Off
- Reassess and repeat if needed

### Maintenance Protocol

After initial healing course:
- 3 days per week at standard dose
- Continue indefinitely for chronic conditions
- Or use PRN (as needed) for flare-ups

### No Cycling Required?

Some practitioners argue BPC-157 does not require cycling because:
- No receptor downregulation has been demonstrated
- No tachyphylaxis observed in animal studies
- It is a naturally occurring compound (derived from gastric juice)

However, cycling remains the conservative standard of care until long-term human safety data is available.

## Combination Protocols

### BPC-157 + TB-500 (The Healing Stack)

The most popular peptide combination for tissue repair:

| Peptide | Dose | Frequency | Duration |
|---------|------|-----------|----------|
| BPC-157 | 250-500 mcg | Daily | 4-8 weeks |
| TB-500 | 2.5 mg | 2x/week (loading), then 1x/week | 4-8 weeks |

**Rationale:** BPC-157 promotes angiogenesis and local repair while TB-500 reduces inflammation and promotes cellular migration. They work synergistically through complementary mechanisms [5].

### BPC-157 + GHK-Cu (Tissue Remodeling)

For post-surgical recovery and scar reduction:
- BPC-157: 250-500 mcg daily (subcutaneous near site)
- GHK-Cu: Topical application to wound/scar area

### BPC-157 + Semaglutide (GI Protection)

For patients experiencing GLP-1 GI side effects:
- BPC-157: 250 mcg oral, 30 min before breakfast
- Continue throughout GLP-1 therapy
- Reduces nausea, protects gastric mucosa

## Contraindications & Precautions

### Absolute Contraindications

- **Active cancer** — BPC-157 promotes angiogenesis which could theoretically support tumor blood supply
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding** — insufficient safety data
- **Known hypersensitivity** to BPC-157 or any component

### Relative Contraindications (Use with Caution)

- History of cancer (within 5 years) — discuss risk/benefit
- Active infection at injection site
- Bleeding disorders or anticoagulant therapy
- Children under 18 (no pediatric data)

### Drug Interactions

BPC-157 has no well-documented drug interactions, but theoretical considerations include:
- May enhance effects of blood pressure medications (via NO modulation)
- May interact with drugs metabolized via the dopaminergic system
- Monitor patients on anticoagulants for injection site bleeding

## Patient Education Points

Provide patients with these key instructions:

1. **Injection technique:** Demonstrate proper subcutaneous injection at first visit
2. **Storage:** Refrigerate immediately after reconstitution, discard after 28 days
3. **Timing:** Inject at the same time daily for consistency
4. **Side effects to report:** Unusual pain, swelling, fever, or allergic symptoms
5. **Expectations:** Most patients notice improvement within 1-2 weeks for acute injuries, 4-6 weeks for chronic conditions
6. **Activity:** Continue normal activity unless otherwise instructed; BPC-157 works best when the tissue is being used (promotes functional healing)

## Monitoring & Follow-Up

### Initial Follow-Up (2 weeks)

- Assess injection technique compliance
- Evaluate for side effects
- Check pain/function improvement (use validated scales)
- Adjust dose if needed

### Mid-Course (4 weeks)

- Reassess clinical response
- Decide whether to continue, adjust, or add TB-500
- Imaging if appropriate (ultrasound for tendon/ligament)

### End of Course (6-8 weeks)

- Final assessment of outcomes
- Decision on maintenance vs. discontinuation
- Plan for future flare-ups

## Quality & Sourcing Considerations

The single most important factor in BPC-157 efficacy and safety is product quality:

- **Source from 503A or 503B pharmacies** whenever possible
- **Require Certificate of Analysis (COA)** with purity >98%
- **Verify peptide content** matches label claim
- **Check for endotoxin testing** (should be <0.5 EU/mg)
- **Avoid research-grade products** for clinical use

## The Bottom Line

BPC-157 remains one of the most versatile and well-tolerated peptides in clinical practice. Its dual-route administration (oral and injectable), excellent safety profile, and broad therapeutic applications make it a cornerstone of regenerative medicine protocols.

Key takeaways for practitioners:
- Standard dose: 250-500 mcg/day for most indications
- Inject near the injury for localized conditions
- Use oral for GI conditions
- Cycle 4 weeks on / 2 weeks off (conservative approach)
- Combine with TB-500 for enhanced healing
- Always source from verified, quality-tested suppliers
